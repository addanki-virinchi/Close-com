Questions to ask before choosing a CRM

A CRM solution should streamline your sales and marketing operations, fit your business needs and offer the best customer experience. Here are a few questions to help you choose the best CRM software for you:

  • When you compare customer relationship management software, you must consider scalability. You may be a small business or a startup now, but a reliable CRM should fuel growth for businesses of all sizes, from SMBs to large corporates. To prevent having to switch tools a year or two down the road, see what kinds of features are available across all plans, not just the one you’re interested in now. Popular CRM features include sales automation, lead scoring, email marketing and a selection of add-ons.

    Tip: Check out a company’s case studies to see if and how they’ve helped businesses like yours grow.

  • If a software error might cost you a big deal, you can’t afford to wait five working days for an email response. Look for a tool with a solid reputation that offers live chat or phone support during your business’ operating hours.

    Tip: Look for a CRM that offers 24/7 support and a handy help desk to be sure it covers your time zone and weekend shifts.

  • Compared to CRM alternatives like spreadsheets and pen and paper, most CRM systems require some learning curve to suss out. Look for an easy CRM platform that allows you to get more done in less time. The key is finding one that’s powerful and feature-rich yet intuitive. Tools with all the bells and whistles may seem great at first, but if they take your team months to get the hang of, that’s a lot of lost time and productivity.

    Tip: To see how easy users find a tool, read reviews on sites like Capterra.

  • Every CRM will need some customization to make it fit your business and sales process. Creating things like custom fields and sales pipelines is standard, but does it offer automation tools to support your customer lifecycle? There are myriad solutions to automate data entry, generating new leads and other day-to-day sales activities. Look at CRM pricing comparisons to see how much you’ll pay for the customizations you need with different providers.

    Tip: Browse a CRM’s app marketplace to see if it connects with your other tools.

  • Sales reports are crucial for improving your results and motivating your team. Look for a CRM that can pull customer information and generate reports without manual input. Even better, look for a tool with real-time dashboards to keep you on track toward goals. When reports are beautiful and easy to make, you might also find yourself using them more.

    Tip: Establish a set of essential report metrics you need to track, then look for them in CRMs.

7 Red Flags To Watch For When You Compare CRMs

Sometimes, the don’ts are just as useful as the dos. Below are a few warning signs to look out for:

  • Cheaper, lesser-known CRM alternatives may seem like a great idea in the short term. However, if your system crashes, your business suffers the cost. Copycats mimic the design of trusted software, are low or non-existent on review sites and have few followers or negative attention on social media.

  • Having help when you need it is essential. Before subscribing, put a company to the test by messaging its customer support via email, live chat or social media. If your sales reps wait too long for an answer, it probably won’t be helpful when your company’s processes are on the line.

  • When conducting your CRM comparison, look for a cloud-based CRM tool that updates automatically and backs up your data online. This is more secure than offline software that requires manual updating and is liable to lose client data.

  • To get an idea of the level of innovation that goes into a CRM tool, take a peek at its company announcements and product updates. You’ll easily find this info on its blog, homepage or other landing pages. The frequency of updates will tell you just the amount of care put into the product.

  • CRM tools should provide an uncluttered and accessible view of your data. Check out product demo videos or screenshots to preview the CRM’s ease of use – the clearer the dashboard looks, the better, and the more likely you and your team is to benefit from the improved visibility.

  • Take time to research how a company treats its staff and partners to understand its values. Employee scandals, licensing feuds and shady practices are signs that a company isn’t headed for success, and its products may reflect this.

  • When it’s time to compare CRMs, customer reviews can be a good source of objective feedback on a product’s functionality and a company’s outlook compared to that brand’s marketing materials. Pay attention to how the company responds to negative reviews to determine how valid the reviewer’s concerns are and how much the brand cares.
